💎 Value Propositions
  • For Homeowners: Same-day response, transparent pricing, experienced local technicians, and digital job tracking.
  • For Commercial Clients: Maintenance contracts, multi-unit service logs, VAT-compliant invoicing, and clear material cost tracking.
  • For the Internal Team: End-to-end workflow from inquiry to ledger — no more spreadsheets or lost paperwork.
  • For Future SaaS Customers: An affordable, Philippines-compliant FSM tool with local support, VAT handling, and offline-capable mobile ops.
The core promise: "Professional service quality with the speed and price of a local shop — backed by software transparency."
❤️ Customer Relationships
  • Personal assistance: Phone callback within hours of inquiry submission.
  • Self-service: Public website allows 24/7 booking and service browsing.
  • Automated follow-up: Status updates via SMS (planned) as jobs progress.
  • Loyalty / AM contracts: Annual maintenance agreements with scheduled reminders.
  • Community trust: Local neighborhood presence, word-of-mouth, and transparent review solicitation.
Relationship model shifts from transactional repair to trusted household service partner.
📢 Channels
  • Public marketing site (SEO + inquiry form)
  • Phone & walk-in (traditional, high-trust)
  • Facebook / local social (community presence)
  • Referrals from existing customers
  • Real estate developer partnerships (B2B)
  • Future: FieldOps marketplace / app store
Digital channel (site) captures leads while sleeping; phone/walk-in closes high-value jobs.
👥 Customer Segments
  • Residential HVAC: Homeowners in Iloilo City needing installation, cleaning, or repair.
  • Residential Electronics: Households with broken TVs, monitors, or appliances.
  • Small Commercial: Offices, sari-sari stores, and small restaurants with 2–10 units.
  • Property Managers: Subdivision admins and landlords needing bulk or recurring maintenance.
  • Future SaaS: Philippine SME service businesses (plumbing, electrical, cleaning) needing affordable FSM.
Segment priority: Residential HVAC (highest margin, highest frequency) → Commercial contracts (recurring revenue) → SaaS (scale).
💰 Revenue Streams
  • Service labor fees (aircon repair, installation, electronics repair)
  • Material markup (parts, refrigerant, capacitors, filters)
  • Annual maintenance contracts (recurring, predictable)
  • FieldOps SaaS subscriptions (monthly per-technician or per-company)
  • Training & onboarding (for SaaS customers)
Revenue mix target: 70% service labor, 20% materials, 10% SaaS by Year 3.
📉 Cost Structure
  • Technician labor & commissions (largest cost)
  • Parts & inventory procurement
  • Vehicle fuel & maintenance
  • Cloud infrastructure (Supabase + Vercel — low, usage-based)
  • Marketing & site hosting
  • Software development (internal team time)
Low fixed-cost architecture: cloud bills scale with usage, not headcount.

Deep Dive: Interconnections

How the nine blocks reinforce each other to create a durable business.

🔁 The Flywheel

More jobs → more data in FieldOps → better scheduling & inventory accuracy → faster turnaround → happier customers → more referrals → more jobs. The software turns operational data into a competitive advantage.

🛡️ Moat Building

Informal technicians cannot replicate a system that tracks every inquiry, job, part, and peso. As FieldOps matures, the gap between J.R.R. and unorganized competitors widens — not through price, but through reliability and transparency.

📈 SaaS Optionality

By dogfooding FieldOps internally, the company validates features before offering them to external users. The service business becomes a paid R&D lab for the software product, de-risking the SaaS pivot.

🇵🇭 Local Compliance Edge

Foreign FSM tools do not handle Philippine VAT rules, lack local payment integrations, and are priced in USD. FieldOps is built for VAT inclusive/exclusive toggles, Philippine invoicing norms, and peso-denominated cost structures.

💡 Asset-Light Scaling

Cloud infrastructure (Supabase + Vercel) means FieldOps can onboard new users with near-zero marginal cost. The service business funds the software; the software eventually funds geographic expansion without proportional headcount growth.

🎯 Grant Alignment

The business aligns with DTI and DOST digitalization mandates: SME tech adoption, local job creation (technicians), and exportable software IP. Grant funding accelerates the SaaS module completion without diluting equity.
