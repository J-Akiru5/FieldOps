Slide 02
The Problem

Philippine Service SMEs Are Flying Blind

Millions of small service businesses — HVAC, plumbing, electrical, appliance repair — operate without modern tools. The result is leakage, chaos, and stunted growth.

📉

No Profit Visibility

Most technicians cannot tell if a job was profitable after deducting parts, fuel, and labor. Spreadsheets are error-prone and rarely used.

📋

Lost Inquiries

Phone and walk-in inquiries are jotted on paper or memorized. Follow-ups are forgotten. Revenue leaks through the cracks every week.

💸

Foreign Software Too Expensive

Global FSM tools charge $40–100/user/month in USD, lack Philippine VAT support, and require credit cards local SMEs do not have.

"We fix air conditioners all day, but we have no idea which customer types make us money, which parts run out too fast, or whether we should hire another tech."

— Typical service business owner, Iloilo City

Slide 03
The Solution

FieldOps: Built by a Technician, for Technicians

An end-to-end field service management platform that connects the front desk, the field technician, and the bookkeeper in one affordable, Philippines-ready system.

  • Inquiry-to-Job Pipeline — Capture leads from web, phone, or walk-in; track status through to conversion.
  • Smart Scheduling — Assign technicians, reserve inventory, and confirm appointments automatically.
  • Job Costing & VAT — Auto-calculate gross, material cost, net profit, and VAT inclusive / exclusive per job.
  • Inventory Tracking — Real-time stock levels with low-stock alerts and movement audit trails.
  • General Ledger — Income, operating expense, and partner draw entries with soft-void audit support.
  • Mobile-Ready Ops — PWA dashboard runs on any phone or tablet for field updates.

Architecture at a Glance

Frontend Next.js 15 + Tailwind + shadcn/ui
Backend & Auth Supabase (Postgres + RLS + Auth)
ORM & Schema Prisma (11 models, production-ready)
Hosting Vercel (edge, auto-scaling)
Monorepo Turborepo + pnpm workspaces

Unit Economics — Service Job

Average ticket: ₱2,500–₱8,000. Material cost: ~30%. Labor cost: ~25%. Net margin per job: ~35–45%. FieldOps tracking improves this by reducing no-shows and stockouts.

Unit Economics — SaaS

Target price: ₱2,500/month per company (3–5 users). Cloud cost per tenant: ~₱400/month. Gross margin: ~84%. Payback on dev cost at 20 paying customers.
