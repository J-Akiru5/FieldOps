Company Overview

From Field Roots to Digital Scale

J.R.R. Air-conditioning and Refrigeration Services is a family-operated field service business headquartered in Iloilo City, Philippines. For years, the company has delivered residential and commercial cooling solutions under the leadership of founder Jose T. Puig, while the electronics repair division is led by Jeremy D. Martinez.

In 2025, the company launched Syntaxure FieldOps — a purpose-built Field Service Management (FSM) system with integrated job-costing, inventory tracking, and sales ledger. Originally developed for internal use, FieldOps is architected as a scalable SaaS product for the underserved Philippine SME service market.

Unlike generic FSM tools that are expensive and foreign-hosted, FieldOps is designed for local compliance (VAT handling, Philippine invoicing norms), offline-capable mobile workflows, and low-cost deployment via Supabase and Vercel.

Strategy

Strategic Placement

Where J.R.R. sits in the market and how it wins.

🎯 Market Position

  • Only integrated HVAC + electronics repair shop in Happy Homes / Zamaga area with a digital booking platform.
  • Competes against informal technicians on trust (transparent pricing, job tracking) and speed (same-day response).
  • Competes against large city firms on local responsiveness and price accessibility.
  • FieldOps software creates a moat: operational efficiency that informal competitors cannot replicate.

📊 Competitive Landscape

  • Informal technicians: Cheaper but unreliable, no warranty, no tracking.
  • City HVAC franchises: Professional but expensive, slow, no local presence.
  • Generic SaaS FSM tools: Foreign-priced, no Philippine VAT support, no offline mode.
  • FieldOps advantage: Built by a local operator for local operators — compliant, affordable, mobile-ready.

🚀 Growth Vectors

  • Service expansion: Add commercial maintenance contracts and annual service agreements.
  • Product expansion: License FieldOps to other Philippine SME service businesses (plumbing, electrical, cleaning).
  • Geographic expansion: Replicate the dual-division model in Bacolod, Roxas, and Kalibo.
  • Parts retail: Sell consumables (filters, refrigerant, capacitors) via the platform.

🛡️ Risk Mitigation

  • Revenue diversification: Two service divisions + software licensing reduces single-sector dependency.
  • Low burn architecture: Supabase + Vercel stack minimizes infrastructure costs.
  • Local supply chain: Inventory tracking prevents over-ordering and stockouts.
  • Data ownership: All customer and financial data stays in-company via self-managed Postgres.

💡 Technology Edge

  • Monorepo architecture (Turborepo + pnpm) enables rapid feature rollout across site and ops apps.
  • Supabase Row-Level Security ensures customer data is tenant-safe if multi-tenancy is introduced.
  • Prisma schema is production-ready for Jobs, Inventory, Sales, and Ledger — not just inquiries.
  • PWA-ready ops dashboard allows technicians to update jobs from mobile devices.

🤝 Partnership Opportunities

  • Appliance brands: Authorized service center partnerships for warranty repairs.
  • Real estate developers: Bulk installation and maintenance contracts for new subdivisions.
  • Local government: Energy-efficiency retrofit programs for public buildings.
  • DTI / DOST: Grant-funded technology adoption and SME digitalization programs.
